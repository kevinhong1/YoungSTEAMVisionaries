FAQ

General FAQ

Our STEAM programs are designed for children aged 5-12, divided into three age groups:
  • Ages 5-7: Introduction to STEAM through interactive play
  • Ages 8-10: Hands-on STEAM activities and fundamental principles
  • Ages 11-12: Advanced STEAM concepts and project-based learning

Our STEAM programs are completely FREE! We believe in making quality STEAM education accessible to all children. This program is funded by donations and run by dedicated volunteers.

Our virtual sessions are conducted over Zoom and Google Meets and include:
  • Interactive online demonstrations
  • Guided hands-on activities using household materials
  • Group discussions and collaborative projects

To participate in our virtual sessions, you'll need:
  • A reliable internet connection
  • A device with a camera and microphone (computer, tablet, or smartphone)
  • Zoom or Google Meet installed
  • A quiet space for learning

We design our activities around common household items. We may also give out worksheets so have a printer handy as well.

Tutoring FAQ

We offer free tutoring in all STEAM-related subjects from K-12

Our online tutoring sessions are:
  • Conducted over Zoom or Google Meet
  • One-on-one or small group sessions available
  • Interactive with digital whiteboards and learning tools
  • Flexible scheduling to match student availability
  • Completely free of charge

Tutoring sessions may vary depending on a student's needs and perferences, but they are usually are around an hour long.

Becoming an Educator FAQ

Our student-educators should have:
  • Strong academic background in STEAM subjects
  • High school student (Grade 9-12) or college student
  • Passion for teaching and helping others
  • Reliable internet connection and computer
  • Commitment to at least one semester of volunteering

The application process includes:
  • Online application form submission
  • Brief video introduction
  • Virtual interview with our team
  • Training session for selected candidates
  • Trial teaching session
